A Girl Who Suffered Greatly From Alopecia Now Makes Profit Out of Her Condition

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, July 16, 2024

Alopecia sounds like a problem for many women who face hair loss. But not for Chloe Marie Bean, an influencer who not only faced this condition head on, but who also managed to turn it into her strength. For Bean, going bald doesn’t sound like a nightmare anymore, and she proves it with her unique success story.

Alopecia changed Chloe Marie Bean’s life completely.

Chloe Marie Bean was born and raised in Midland, Michigan and now lives in Tempe, Arizona. But when she was diagnosed with alopecia at the age of 8, her perspective on life changed completely.

Alopecia is an autoimmune disease that causes the immune system to attack the hair follicles, which results in severe hair loss. From this condition, Bean has gone bald 4 times, yet she admits that she wouldn’t change a thing.

The woman’s condition taught her many important lessons.

Chloe Bean’s alopecia journey has not been easy. But, as she confessed herself, she’s learned the importance of self-love through her special condition. “Loving who you are and embracing things that are far out of your control makes you unique, powerful, and beautiful,” says the woman.

Embracing Bean’s condition wasn’t easy. In an interview, she recalls, “I have been bald 3 times in my life now, and I don’t really remember my initial reaction the first time I went bald since I was so young, but what I do remember is feeling like I didn’t belong when I was around people in public or around my friends since I wasn’t like the other girls who had such long, beautiful hair. Instead, I always had to wear hats out in public to try and hide the bald spots that were scattered throughout my hair.”

Going completely bald inspired the woman to start her own business.

Family means everything to Bean. Her cousins saw how difficult it was for her growing up at times without hair. So when she recently went bald again, her cousins grew out their own hair and gifted her a wig from her family’s locks.

This became an inspiration for the woman to start her own business. Today, Chloe Bean owns Baldie Bean Beauty, an online platform where she sells wigs and other accessories for women who happen to have alopecia.

The woman has become a global inspiration for those who used to think that going bald meant not being beautiful anymore. Bean has proven that hair loss doesn’t have to break a woman’s spirit and self-confidence.
