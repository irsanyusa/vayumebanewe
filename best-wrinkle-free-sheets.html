Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, September 13, 2024

While ironing your sheets is a foolproof way to smooth them, who has that kind of time? What to consider when buying wrinkle-free sheets

If you’re looking for sheets that are not only wrinkle-free, but also comfortable and stylish, the options can be overwhelming. Here are some of the most important factors to consider when searching for the best bed sheets available on the market.

Consider the material

Perhaps the most important factor in choosing any sheet set is selecting the right material for your particular needs. (Materials affect thread count and you can learn more about that in our guide on the best thread count for sheets.) Some materials, like eucalyptus and bamboo, are naturally cooling and breathable, with a silkier texture, while other types of materials, like cotton, are known for being more crisp to the touch, and tend to get softer with each wash. So, if your priority is to find the best cooling sheet that’s also wrinkle-resistant, you might focus your search on eucalyptus and bamboo. But if you prefer the crisp feel of cotton, yet still want a wrinkle-free experience, read on for our next consideration: the weave.

Consider the weave

The weave refers to the way the actual fibers are constructed in your bedding. Some of the most common weaves are percale, sateen, twill, and jersey. A sateen weave on a cotton sheet will typically make the sheet more soft and supple, which can reduce the appearance of wrinkles, while a percale weave will give you more strength and durability. Twill is also known for durability, but it’s often flagged as less comfortable than other weaves. A jersey weave, on the other hand, is known for its stretch and softness.

Naturally wrinkle-free vs. chemically treated

When shopping for wrinkle-free sheets, keep an eye out for sheets that have been treated with chemicals like formaldehyde—these chemicals can temporarily keep sheets smooth, but they can also be harmful to your health. Instead, opt for sheets made of naturally wrinkle-resistant fabrics, like eucalyptus, wool, or microfiber. You can also keep an eye out for quality certifications, like OEKO-TEX®, which means products have been tested for a list of up to 350 harmful chemicals.

If your sheets are constantly emerging from your dryer wrinkled like an accordion, the culprit may be an overstuffed dryer. When your dryer is overfull, your sheets won’t dry evenly, which can lead to wrinkling. To fix this issue, simply fill up your machine halfway with bedsheets, or at most three-fourths of the way full. This will allow them to move around while drying, which will lead to a more even drying process and less wrinkles.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the best material for sheets to not wrinkle?

If you’re looking to avoid chemicals in your bedding while still keeping your sleep surface smooth, then you’ll want to look for materials that are naturally wrinkle-resistant. Eucalyptus sheets are a great choice, as they’re not only naturally wrinkle-resistant, they also have a silky texture and are naturally breathable and cooling. Microfiber, made from combed polyester, is another material that tends to be wrinkle-resistant by nature.

What sheets are less likely to wrinkle?

Sheets are less likely to wrinkle if they’re made from wrinkle-resistant fabrics or materials, like eucalyptus, bamboo, microfiber, or sheets with a sateen weave. Sheets may also be less likely to wrinkle if they have been treated with wrinkle-resistant chemicals. 

Do dryer sheets actually prevent wrinkles?

They can! Dryer sheets are squares of fabric soaked in fabric softener. As the dryer heats up, the fabric softener is released onto your clothing. This not only helps your clothes (and sheets) smell fresh, it can also reduce static, leading to less wrinkling.

How often should you wash your sheets?

Washing your sheets every two weeks or so is standard practice, and will help prevent buildup of dust and bacteria. But if you find that your sheets are unsightly or wrinkled after one week, then washing them weekly might be your best bet. To learn more on this topic, read our guide to how often you should wash your sheets.

How do you unwrinkle sheets quickly?

The best and quickest way to smooth a wrinkled sheet is to iron it. But if you don’t have time for that, there are a few other tricks you can try. One method is to take your bedding out of the dryer several times during the dry cycle to shake it, which helps keep wrinkles at bay during the drying process. Another trick is to pull the fitted sheet taut on your mattress and mist it lightly with water from a spray bottle. As the water dries, the sheet will already be in a flat, smooth position, so it will be more likely to dry smooth and wrinkle-free.
