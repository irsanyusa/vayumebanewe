GTBank Founder Fola Adeola, Others Make Tinubus Ambassadorial List, Details Emerge

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, July 4, 2024
  • President Bola Ahmed Tinubu is currently reviewing the ambassadorial list and has set up a committee to send in more qualified candidates
  • However, from the list on Tinubu's table, the founder of GTBank, Tajudeen Afolabi Adeola, was favoured
  • According to close sources, Adeola will be designated Nigeria's ambassador to one of the world's top three economies

Legit.ng journalist Esther Odili has over two years of experience covering political parties and movements.

State House, Abuja - President Bola Ahmed Tinubu has received a list of prospective Nigerian Ambassadors.

As reported by the Punch, the list containing the names of the ambassadors to various countries is currently before President Tinubu for consideration.

President Tinubu is carefully considering the list of potential ambassadors, with a particular focus on deploying individuals with a strong background in banking and industry to represent Nigeria on the global stage.

A top official in Nigeria's foreign service disclosed that one of those being considered is the founder of Guaranty Trust Bank (GTB), Tajudeen Afolabi Adeola.

The source stated:

“Yes, the President has the list and is considering it already. In fact, one of those I saw on the list is Fola Adeola.”

Why Tinubu picked technocrats for ambassadors

The source explained the rationale for the president's choice, saying Tinubu's administration was keen on deploying technocrats and captains of industry as its front persons in foreign lands.

With a global diplomatic network comprising 109 missions, 76 embassies, 22 high commissions, and 11 consulates, Nigeria is undergoing a diplomatic reshuffle.

This move reflects the administration's commitment to strengthening Nigeria's international relations and leveraging the expertise of individuals like Adeola for key diplomatic responsibilities.

Tinubu recalls Nigeria's ambassadors

Earlier, Legit.ng reported that President Tinubu directed the recall of all career and non-career ambassadors serving in different countries on September 2, 2023.

Yusuf Tuggar, minister of foreign affairs, announced the decision barely 24 hours after the president recalled Sarafa Isola, Nigeria's High Commissioner to the United Kingdom.

In a statement by Alkasim Abdulkadir, his media aide, the minister said the recall of Isola on Friday, September 1, applied to all ambassadors.

Tinubu sacks 5 Buhari's appointees in the aviation sector

In another development, Legit.ng earlier reported that President Tinubu also sacked five directors appointed by Buhari in the aviation and aerospace development ministry.

Some of the affected directors were appointed a few days before the handing over of the former president to Tinubu.

While listing the affected directors, the spokesperson of the ministry, Odutayo Oluseyi, said legal advisers and secretaries were not affected by the directives.
